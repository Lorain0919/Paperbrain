# Paper Information

## Title
Mitochondrial Stress in H. pylori Infection

## Type
Review Article

---

# Background

Helicobacter pylori infection induces mitochondrial dysfunction and oxidative stress, contributing to gastric inflammation and carcinogenesis.

---

# Objective

To summarize the relationship between H. pylori infection, mitochondrial stress, mitophagy, and inflammatory signaling.

---

# Main Findings

## ROS

- H. pylori increases mitochondrial ROS production.
- Excessive ROS contributes to cellular damage.

## Mitophagy

- Damaged mitochondria can be removed through mitophagy.
- PINK1/Parkin pathway plays an important role.

## NLRP3 Inflammasome

- Mitochondrial dysfunction can activate NLRP3.
- Mitophagy may suppress excessive inflammasome activation.
